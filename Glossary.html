Glossary

Inflation An increase in the price levels of goods and services in an economy

Hyperinflation when demand suddenly and excessively outpaces supply, causing prices to rise by more than 50% month over month.

High Inflation when demand outpaces supply. Inflation rates about 2% month over month are considered high.

Deflation the downward general trend in the cost of goods and services

Stagflation a phenomena in which high inflation in combined with high unemployment

Cost-push inflation decrease in the supply of goods and services due to external factors (war, natural disaster etc)

Demand pull inflation when demand for goods and services increases faster than the supply

Expectations of future inflation when people expect that prices will increase in the future, and in response demand higher wages pushing up production costs

Money supply the total amount of money in circulation in an economy

Consumer price index (CPI) a measure of the average price of a selection of sample goods and services consumed by households only

General price index a measure of the average price of a selection of sample goods and services consumed by households, businesses, governments, and foreigners

Seigniorage the power of governments to print more money
